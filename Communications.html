Use of the Internet

There are many useful ways to use the Internet. The internet has introduced a new way of communication, education, and social interaction. E-mailing and instant messaging are now part of our everyday life. Looking for answers or definitions no more requires heavy dictionaries; various search engines will find answers in milliseconds. The latest news is available instantly without the need for a TV or newspaper.

In 2021 education has moved online and it is easier than ever to find information and education materials and tutorials. 

Online shopping is more popular than ever, mostly because high street shops are closed during the pandemic. More and more local shops offer home delivery and online shopping. Most popular online shops in Ireland are Littlewoods, Boohoo, Asos, Komplett and Amazon UK.

Online banking and shopping online have made shopping from home very accessible, especially in the middle of a pandemic. Internet is changed how we pay bills and do our banking. I am using online banking a lot and it gives great control, like managing my direct debits and sending/receiving money.

Movies and music are distributed mostly online for a fixed monthly subscription fee. The most popular video streaming companies are Netflix, Prime Video, and Disney Plus. The most popular music streaming services are Spotify, Apple Music, and Tidal.

Microsoft launched Xbox Game Pass on 1 June 2017. (Humphries, 2021) It has grown in a very successful subscription business model. It allows download games on Xbox game console, Personal Computer or stream games to android mobile phone. (xbox.com, 2020)

Job searching has become easier as most vacancies are advertised online. During the recent pandemic, many employees are working from home.  As good as it sounds working from home comes with the negative side of longer working hours. The research by Laya Healthcare found that 65% of those working from home feel pressure to stay connected after normal hours.  Employees worked an average of 22 hours of overtime per month over the last year. Peoples mental health is suffering from being always-on. (McHugh, 2021)
